The famous Sakura in Japan

20 April 2016 on japan, sakura, cherryblossom

Cherry blossom in full blossom in Japan. I cannot simply think of a better feast for the eyes. Its spectacular. Despite of annual its occurrence locals celebrate cherry blossom to the fullest. You will see natives enjoying Hanami - a centuries old Japanese tradition of picnicking under sakura everywhere.

You Want to visit Japan for cherry blossom.

Well, who don't want to? Obviously you need little planning early. You get a 2-3 month window. But flowers only bloom for less than 2 weeks. Fully bloomed span is even less. So area is the key. Choose which area in Japan you visit wisely. I'm trying to share my experience with planning and viewing Sakura.

When Should I visit Japan to experience Sakura bloom?

It depends on place of visit. There are predictions for the bloom based on the area.
JNTO Cherry Blossom Predictions is a very good source to track predictions. Flowers only bloom for less than 2 weeks. You got to be lucky to plan a trip early for the full bloom. Typically, It begins on southern islands of Okinawa in late January. As temperature rises it move towards north.

Is hotels and air tickets expensive during the season?

Of course. Hotels can be fully booked if you wait until last week. So follow the predictions and plan early. Even Airbnb listings becomes expensive and rare during season.

What are the best locations?

Tokyo remains a very popular Sakura viewing destination. But all over the Japan is blanketed with flowers. Again choose the location based on the time you visit. Followings are some snaps I captured at Tokyo.
